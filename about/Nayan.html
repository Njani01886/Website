Summary

I am currently a Data Analytics and Computational Social Science Masters student at UMass Amherst. As a Social Science Data Analyst, I am passionate about understanding the social, political, and economic processes that generate real world data and providing data-driven insights that improve business decisions and lives of other people using language that can be easily interpreted.

Throughout my academic and professional journey, I’ve developed strong skills in data analysis, statistics, and machine learning. I’ve worked on projects ranging from predictive modeling to social media sentiment analysis, and I enjoy using my skills to uncover findings and patterns that can inform decision-making and drive positive change.

Additionally, I have completed several projects that required me to collect, clean, and analyze large sets of data, which provided me with hands-on experience with data cleaning and scraping, feature engineering, model selection, and evaluation using tools such as SQL, R, and Python. My previous internship at Bora, a startup company, enabled me to work with a variety of stakeholders, providing me with experience in working collaboratively, which I believe is essential for success.

As a person, I’m motivated by a desire to help others. I believe that data can be a powerful force for improving the quality of life of any individual, and I’m committed to using my skills to help businesses and individuals achieve their goals. I always strive to be supportive, empathetic, and collaborative when working with others, and I enjoy working with people from diverse backgrounds and perspectives.

If you’re interested in connecting with me or learning more about my work, please don’t hesitate to reach out.
